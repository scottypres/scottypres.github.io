Instructions:

Edit Wind Speed Thresholds: This feature lets you customize the color-coding for various wind speeds on the visualization. By assigning specific RGB colors to defined wind speed ranges, you can create a personalized, multi-level view of wind conditions across different altitudes over an extended time frame. The primary objective of this webpage is to offer an intuitive and interactive method to graphically represent and monitor wind patterns that are critical for flight planning.
Wind/Clouds Toggle: By clicking this button, you can switch the view between displaying the wind speed/direction and the cloud cover information. This allows you to assess the flying conditions more comprehensively by considering both wind behavior at different altitudes and cloud coverage.
Cloud Cover Representation: Cloud cover is visualized with varying shades of grey in the cells. Darker shades represent heavier cloud cover, while lighter shades indicate lighter cloud cover or clear skies.
Temperature Toggle: Use this button to toggle between viewing wind/cloud data and temperature data across different altitudes. This feature helps you to visualize the temperature conditions and make informed decisions for your flight.
Enable/Disable Wind Shear: Wind shear can present significant hazards to flight. This button allows you to enable an analysis which visually indicates where wind shear conditions are detected based on the threshold settings for wind speed and direction change. They are 10mph and 90 degrees. This analysis is a work in progress and is only applied to ICON data and altitudes up to 6000ft.
Daylight/Night Toggle: This functionality filters the displayed data to show only the daylight hours (calculated from sunrise sunset values and column headers circled in orange). Piloting during dark hours can be risky, so you may use this filter to focus on the relevant flight times.
Show High Altitude: You can toggle the visibility of high altitude data by clicking this button. If you're interested in flying at higher elevations, this can help you plan ahead and avoid potentially dangerous zones.
Additional Variables Toggle: This feature adds or removes additional weather variables such as Convective Available Potential Energy (CAPE) or precipitation probability, which can be crucial for understanding developing weather conditions that might affect your flight. Precipitation probability is only available for the ICON model.
Best Hours: Upon clicking this button, a popup will appear allowing you to input values for maximum wind speed you are comfortable flying in. The system will then highlight the hours in which wind speeds are below this threshold at 0-400ft. This does not yet consider precipitation probability.
Wind Arrows: Wind direction is indicated by arrows within the data cells. These arrows rotate to point in the direction the wind is going.
